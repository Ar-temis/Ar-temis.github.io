Most of the books here are for system architecture, patterns, and OOP design.
If you are a student who is confused about what to learn after your `Introduction to Data Structures` course, these are helpful sources to learn
software engineering concepts without the buzzwords.

## Data Representation & Storage

- [Designing Data Intensive Applications](https://learning.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-data-intensive-applications/9781098119058/) by Martin Kleppmann and Chris Riccomini

    Critically acclaimed book that is often cited as one of the must-read books for SWE. Talks about, data storage, how DBs work, how data is transferred, distributed system connection, etc.


## SWE Philosophy

- [No Silver Bullets](https://courses.cs.duke.edu/compsci408/fall25/readings/no_silver_bullet.pdf) by
Frederick P. Brooks, Jr.
    I think the opening sentence explains what this article is about very clearly. I'll spare you the trouble and cite it here:

    > There is no single development, in either technology or management technique,
    > which by itself promises even one order-of-magnitude improvement within a decade
    > in productivity, in reliability, in simplicity.

- [Code tells you how, comment tells you why](https://blog.codinghorror.com/code-tells-you-how-comments-tell-you-why/)
    by Jeff Atwood
