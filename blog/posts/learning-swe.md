As a college student trying to navigate the industry and learn everything that has happened before
my time, AI has been nothing but a discouragement. Talking about AI like this even though I am a
PM at my university's chatbot is way too ironic, but this is what I constantly think:

_Why learn anything at all? AI will do it better than me anyway._

That's the narrative I hear from the students around me and the voice inside of me.

> "The biggest thing that I see that worries me is the junior engineering
> market being completely decimated." - Demetri Spanos

This is happening in the U.S., the leading software engineering market, and I fear that the rest of
the world will follow this trend.

There has to be junior engineers to have senior engineers. If we, fresh out of college juniors,
never painstakingly go through bug hunts, build projects by hand, and eventually take down prod,
we will never gain that insight/intuition of senior engineers.

## Should I Become a Carpenter?

All this hype and exposure to AI makes me feel like I am just born in the wrong era.
Comically, I yearn for the times that are long gone - way before I was born.
Whenever I read computer science books (like [OS: 3 easy pieces](https://www.amazon.com/Operating-Systems-Three-Easy-Pieces/dp/198508659X) or [Designing data intensive applications](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-data-intensive-applications/9781491903063/preface01.html#idm45085131334912), both of which I heavily recommend for any SW Engineer),
I just admire the ingenuity and clever thinking of the engineers before me.
Humans have evolved so much that they are doing arcane tricks with tiny rocks.

Yet, now I find myself in a generation which fails to appreciate those efforts from the forefathers.
They just ask away their silly little questions from the "thinking" machine and fail to grasp the meaning of their question.

_"Hey Claude, build me Claude 2.0"_

I just wish more people cared about what they were doing and producing. If you are
blindly letting some random next word generator make decisions for you and "think"
for you, what are you really? An organic flesh GPT wrapper?

Similarly, hiring managers and big corporations should also notice that this is a tool,
not a human replacement. But capitalism speaks louder in their ears and human nature
gets thrown out of the window.

Recently, it has been harder and harder to drown out "the AI will replace you" narrative, and so
I have decided to delete my social media and just focus on myself. I genuinely believe that if I
keep my head down and study just like how the engineers who never had this stochastic parrot did,
I can somewhat survive the junior to senior pipeline. That, I can gain the software building intuition
I was talking about. Because let's be honest, software will never go away. Software is all about information
and data manipulation, and such things are a digital gold. You can do so much good and so much harm with it.
And I just hope that if I learn that intuition, I can be a sought-after engineer who can choose what good
to do with my knowledge.

## Closing Words

To others who feel the same way as I do, I just wanna say that knowledge has never been so valuable.
If you choose to actually pursue this career, it will always be worth it to take the hard route and
painstakingly learn the nitty gritty concepts. But, who am I to tell you that? After all, I am just a junior
who never had an official job.
